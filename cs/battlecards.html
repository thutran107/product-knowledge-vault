Passthrough
Our #1 competitor in FundSub, especially emerging/mid-market. Two products — digital sub-docs + managed AML/KYC — sold cheap up front and cross-sold downstream. Strong law-firm channel (Goodwin).
OVERLAPS: FUNDSUB · IDM · DATA ROOM
Anduin 6W – 9L
High confidence
Why we win
  • Platform maturity — Apollo's pilot found PT "not mature enough"; won Silver Point despite quoting 2×.
  • UI + SSO — won Lattice Capital on sleek UI + SSO though PT was cheaper.
  • CS quality — Fin Capital ($1B) left PT for poor support & slow response.
  • Conditional logic / digitization depth — PT logic is basic, repeats questions.
Where they're strong
  • Price — one-time, non-recurring per-fund fee "good for the life of the fund."
  • LP UI — "the TurboTax of private markets."
  • In-app AML/KYC + OFAC — ongoing screening; stickier.
  • Goodwin channel — referrals; has pushed to replace us at renewal (2150 VC).
Their pricing — directional deal quotes
  • Emerging fund (FundSub)$5–7.5K
  • General per fundraise$15–20K
  • NYCA — one-time, 3 vehicles (vs Anduin $61.5K)$18K one-time
  • Blue Torch ($7.2B) — negotiated$40K+$7.5K → $20K/yr
  • AML/KYC per investor$75–100/yr
  • 2026 managed sub-doc review$250/LP
Objections → Responses
Passthrough is cheaper — and it's a one-time fee.
Their single strongest lever. Reframe on total cost of ownership: our bundle (FundSub + IDM + Data Room + Portal + IH) replaces multiple tools. Lead with maturity, CS quality, and SSO — not price.
Passthrough does our AML/KYC in-app.
Known loss driver — address head-on. Position Anduin's AML/KYC + managed-services path; note PT's check is human-in-the-loop, not magic.
Our law firm (Goodwin) recommends Passthrough.
Channel-driven, not capability-driven. Counter with references, platform maturity, and LP-experience proof points.

AtomInvest
Broad all-in-one platform (CRM, onboarding, LP reporting, portfolio, accounting). Wider but less focused than Anduin — and dogged by data-integrity and security weaknesses.
OVERLAPS: INVESTOR PORTAL · FUNDSUB · INTEGRATION HUB
Anduin 1W – 3L
Med confidence
Why we win
  • Data integrity — GA dropped them ("data structure not good"); data "becomes unusable" post-execution.
  • Digitization speed — could take them ~a month per sub-doc.
  • QES / DocuSign — they lacked QES, couldn't integrate DocuSign (Redalpine).
  • Security — SSO-bypass password reset; weak 4-digit email MFA.
Where they're strong
  • Lower price at enterprise tier.
  • Broader suite — portfolio management + fund accounting.
  • Distribution — signed J.P. Morgan AM ($3.7T) in 2025.
  • GP/lawyer-side workflow management.
Their pricing — directional deal quotes
  • Per fund$10K setup + $5–10K/yr
  • Indico (FundSub + 1 fund)$20K
  • Blue Torch — enterprise, negotiated$45K+$7.5K → $28.4K/yr
Objections → Responses
AtomInvest is cheaper at our tier.
Bundle TCO favors Anduin. Then pivot to the documented data-integrity and post-execution data-usability problems — that's the durable wedge, not price.
AtomInvest is all-in-one — it has accounting.
Anduin owns the investor-relationship layer with superior data integrity; accounting integrates via Integration Hub. All-in-one ≠ best-in-class at onboarding.

The category leader in side-letter / MFN terms tracking (AI-powered "Insight"). This is our least-mature overlap — an attach/defend motion, not a head-to-head platform fight.

Juniper Square
Investor-management platform strongest with emerging managers and simple structures — now investing heavily in AI (JunieAI, $130M Series D; Headless GPX MCP product).

  • 2 investor buckets only; no investor passport. Fits simple/emerging mgrs.

Position depth on workflow, conditional logic, AML/KYC, and investor passport. JS fits simpler/emerging managers; for complex fund structures we win on rigor. Take their AI roadmap seriously for renewal stickiness.

iCapital & CAIS
The dominant wealth-channel alternatives distribution platform. Overlaps Anduin most on wealth/RIA onboarding and — since acquiring Parallel Markets — reusable investor identity.
OVERLAPS: FUNDSUB · IDM
No head-to-head logged
Med confidence
Why we win / their gaps
  • No direct fund communication; pre-review submission workflows.
  • Can't pre-fill by entity type — an Anduin strength.
  • Advisors creating own co-invest vehicles to dodge iCapital fees — a structural opening.
Where they're strong

We already use iCapital for wealth distribution.
Different layer — they're distribution, we're direct GP↔LP onboarding. Position entity-type prefill, data integrity, and direct fund comms. The co-invest-vehicle trend is a wedge to sell GPs their own tooling.
