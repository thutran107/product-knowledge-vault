## Summary
Export of the **Tracking Table** database from Anduin's Notion "Competitive Intel Repository," covering 153 dated field-intel entries logged by Sales/RM/CS from mid-2022 through June 2026. Each entry captures a single observation about a competitor or market player — pricing data points, win/loss reasons, product-functionality gaps, security findings, partnerships, funding rounds, and rebrands — most sourced from internal Slack threads, customer calls, and public announcements. The dominant competitors by volume are Passthrough (46 entries), AtomInvest (24), Subscribe (16), and Ontra (11).

## Key Takeaways
- **Passthrough is the #1 competitor**, especially in Fundsub for emerging/mid-market GPs. It wins on low price and LP UI ("the TurboTax of private markets") and on its law-firm channel (Goodwin partnership). It loses on maturity, CS support, and at the whale tier. Its strategic play: cheap Fundsub as a funnel, cross-sell into managed AML/KYC.
- **Pricing intel is the highest-value content here** — competitor quotes spanning Passthrough ($10–40K/fund + AML/KYC at $75–100/investor), AtomInvest ($5–10K/fund, $20–28K enterprise), Subscribe ($45K all-in platform incl. managed services + API), and Ontra ($75–80K/yr for side-letter terms tracking).
- **Win/loss patterns are consistent**: Anduin wins on UI/UX, SSO, platform maturity, and end-to-end bundle; loses on price (esp. Passthrough's one-time non-recurring fee), AML/KYC checking, and side-letter tracking (Ontra/Pro-Vision/Carta).
- **Two structural market threats recur**: (1) **fund admins building in-house onboarding** (NAV, Flow→Apex, Dynamo→InvestHub) eroding the Fundsub channel; (2) **AI-native LP-side entrants** (Juniper Square JunieAI, Vantager, Daphne, BlueFlame.ai→Datasite) opening a new competitive surface Anduin doesn't yet cover.
- **Lots of consolidation/rebrand activity (2025–26)**: IDR→Sonata One, BiteStream→Investor Pointe, Intralinks→InvestorVision, iCapital acquiring Parallel Markets, Subscribe launching the Altscape marketplace.
