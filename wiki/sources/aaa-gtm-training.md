## Summary
The GTM enablement deck delivered ahead of the December 2024 MVP release, equipping Sales/RM and CS to pitch the RIA offerings. It frames RIA personas and problems, the three-part RIA offering (Advisor Advantage app, fund-manager collaboration in FundSub, custodian-approved signature), the rollout roadmap, value propositions, target-customer profiles, and ready-to-use enablement assets (slide decks + email template).

## Key Takeaways
- **Scale stat (Oct 2024)**: 630 RIAs working on 13,670 subscriptions on Anduin — cross-checked against Preqin. (More recent than the product brief's 365.)
- **Three RIA offerings**: (1) Anduin Advisor Advantage — dedicated workspace for RIAs; (2) enhanced fund-manager collaboration inside FundSub (advisor invitation, real-time visibility, controls over invited entities); (3) signature enhancements (one-envelope experience + Schwab custodian-approved signing).
- **Roadmap**:
  - *Dec 2024 (MVP)*: RIA app, fund-manager collaboration, Schwab custodian-approved signature.
  - *Jan 2025*: one-envelope signing experience.
  - *2025*: continued enhancements, Fidelity custodian signature, and **pursuing RIAs as paying customers**.
- **Pricing posture (MVP)**: the offering is **not charged at MVP** — the goal is to onboard as many customers as possible to gather insights; pricing for premium features comes later as the offering matures.
- **Auto-fill**: advisors prefill subscription docs from past subscriptions or from an investor passport (Investor Access), and can reuse pre-saved AML/KYC documents.
- **Target fund managers**: experienced working with multiple RIAs recently, want to onboard more, or were previously unable to work with RIAs on Anduin due to old limitations.
- **Enablement assets**: four demo decks (Advisor Advantage, custodian-approved signing, one-envelope) plus a customer email template introducing the three offerings.
