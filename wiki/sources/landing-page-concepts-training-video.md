## Summary
Sales-enablement training on Anduin **Landing Pages** (= the **Branded Landing Pages** feature of [[products/engagement-hub|Engagement Hub]]) — what it is, the GP brand problem it solves, the two-level access model, the three access-granting mechanisms, and how to position/package it. Naming note: the content consistently treats "Landing Pages" as a *feature* available across packages, built **within IDM** — consistent with the vault's deprecation of a standalone Landing Page product.

## Key Takeaways
- **White-label front door:** collapses scattered DocuSign links / Dropbox folders / email PDFs into one branded destination — "the investor never sees the word Anduin." Built within IDM ("the platform that already manages your investors, their fund relationships, and their data room access").
- **Control is the product:** **draft-before-publish** ("nothing is visible to investors until the GP decides to publish").
- **Custom vs. System pages:** **Custom pages** (firm homepage, fund pages) are fully editable and access-gated; **System pages** (Resources, Documents, Inbox, Profile) are auto-populated, always-on, require no upkeep, are color-themeable, and can be **unpublished** if not in use.
- **Two-level access control:** (1) which **pages** a contact can see; (2) which **dynamic content** (subscriptions, data rooms, opportunities) renders inside via that investor's permissions — "same login screen, completely different experience." Dynamic widgets filter in real time.
- **Three access-granting mechanisms:** **direct** (manual per-contact), **invitation link** (shareable, optional password + expiry), and **auto access** (syncs page permissions with investment relationships via the contact matrix — the only one that scales; anchored to reporting pages where stakes are highest).
- **Packaging (sales playbook):** three tiers — **Investor Portal** (recommended; includes auto access + dynamic reporting + full suite), **Engagement Hub** (branded experience without the full suite), and **standalone** (price-sensitive only; steer away). **Published-page caps** on lower tiers are a built-in **upgrade trigger** (home page + system pages don't count toward the limit).
- **Buying signals:** fragmented links, brand anxiety, tiered investor segments, co-branded arrangements.
