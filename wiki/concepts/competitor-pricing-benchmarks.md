## Definition
Consolidated, directional benchmarks of what Anduin's competitors charge, drawn from field intel (deal quotes, ex-employee reports, customer disclosures). These are **individual data points, not list prices** — useful for framing and negotiation, not as guaranteed competitor pricing.

## Key dynamics
- **Passthrough's one-time, non-recurring per-fund fee is the single most disruptive pricing lever** in the market — it reframes the buy as a one-time cost "good for the life of the fund," repeatedly beating Anduin's recurring model in emerging/mid deals.
- **Subscribe bundles managed services + API into a flat platform fee**, collapsing Anduin's itemized quote into one low number.
- **Side-letter pricing (Ontra) is AUM/volume-based and high** ($75–80K/yr) — a different pricing axis from per-fund Fundsub.
- **AML/KYC is increasingly priced per-investor/entity** ($75–100), a recurring attach competitors use to raise stickiness and ACV.

## Tensions & open questions
- Many figures are 2022–2024 and may be stale; competitor pricing has trended toward bundling and managed services.
- Anduin's itemized quoting consistently produces a much larger headline number than competitors' bundled flat fees (e.g., a Subscribe deal: ~$45K vs. Anduin ~$176K). Open question: when to lead with a bundle to neutralize this.
- Competitor one-time-fee models vs. Anduin recurring: how to defend recurring value (CS, maturity, updates) at the emerging tier.
